## 1. Two-Product Architecture

DeepAudit operates as a **premium intelligence platform** purpose-built for PE deal flow. Two products, one buyer, compounding value.

### Product 1: Technical Due Diligence

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **When** | Pre-acquisition (during due diligence) |
| **What** | Full-stack risk assessment — security vulnerabilities, scalability ceilings, reliability gaps, tech debt quantification, AI/ML risk exposure, architecture quality scoring |
| **Buyer** | PE Operating Partner / Deal Team |
| **Output** | PE-ready risk report with investment impact assessment, remediation cost estimate, and negotiation leverage points |
| **Price** | $25,000–$150,000 per assessment |
| **Competitors** | Boutique DD firms ($50K–$200K), Big 4 ($100K–$500K) |

DeepAudit analyzes source code directly — not documents, not questionnaires, not self-reported surveys. Every finding is traceable to a specific file, function, and line number. This is the difference between an opinion and evidence.

### Product 2: Compliance Readiness Audit

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **When** | Post-acquisition (first 90 days of ownership) |
| **What** | 6-framework compliance gap analysis covering SOC2, GDPR, HIPAA, DPDP Act, ISO 27001, and CCPA/CPRA |
| **Buyer** | Same Operating Partner, now managing the portfolio company |
| **Output** | Compliance readiness report per framework with control matrix, gap severity classification, estimated remediation cost, and implementation timeline |
| **Price** | $30,000–$100,000+ per engagement |
| **Competitors** | GRC consultants ($50K–$150K), compliance automation platform setup ($30K–$80K) |

Compliance Readiness is the natural successor to Technical DD. The same operating partner who used DeepAudit to evaluate the acquisition now uses it to prepare the portfolio company for enterprise sales, regulatory requirements, and eventual exit.

### The Flywheel

```
Technical DD → Compliance Readiness → Portfolio Intelligence → Exit Preparation
     │                │                       │                      │
     └── $25K-$150K ──┘── $30K-$100K+ ───────┘── $200K/yr ─────────┘── $75K-$150K
```

**One buyer. Two+ transactions. $55K–$250K+ per deal.** The relationship compounds: every DD engagement creates a natural Compliance Readiness opportunity. Every compliance engagement creates an ongoing monitoring contract. Every monitoring contract feeds the next fund's DD pipeline.

## 2. Premium Pricing Philosophy

Premium pricing is not a marketing tactic — it is a product strategy. These five principles govern every pricing and packaging decision.

### Principle 1: Premium Is Earned Through Proof

We cannot charge $50K for an unproven product. The path to premium:

| Phase | Audits Completed | Pricing | Purpose |
|-------|-----------------|---------|---------|
| **Proof** (Q1–Q2 2026) | 0–20 | $10K–$15K (pilot pricing) | Build case studies, refine methodology, collect testimonials |
| **Validation** (Q3 2026) | 20–50 | $25K–$50K | Published results, repeat customers, referral pipeline |
| **Premium** (Q4 2026+) | 50+ | $50K–$150K | Established brand, waitlist demand, ecosystem lock-in |

### Principle 2: The Product Speaks for Itself

Reports must be visually stunning, technically rigorous, and immediately actionable. No buyer should need a sales call to understand the value. The report IS the sales tool for the next engagement.

- Every report includes an executive summary a non-technical board member can read in 5 minutes
- Every finding links to specific code with severity, business impact, and remediation estimate
- Reports are designed for boardroom presentation — not developer terminals

### Principle 3: No Discounting — Reduce Scope Instead

| Instead of... | Do this... |
|---------------|-----------|
| 30% discount on Full DD | Offer Rapid Screen at $25K (smaller scope, same per-unit value) |
| "Startup pricing" | Offer a single-framework compliance audit at $30K |
| Volume discount | Portfolio Intelligence contract with committed annual spend |

Price integrity preserves brand positioning. Every discount trains the market to negotiate.

### Principle 4: Scarcity Creates Desire

- **Waitlist model:** Accept a limited number of new PE partnerships per quarter
- **Capacity signaling:** "We can take 3 new DD engagements this month" creates urgency
- **Referral priority:** Existing clients' referrals skip the queue

### Principle 5: Ecosystem Lock-In

The full lifecycle creates natural retention:

```
DD Assessment → Compliance Readiness → Quarterly Monitoring → Exit DD → Next Fund
```

Each step makes the next step easier (baseline data, historical trends, established relationship). Switching costs compound over time.

## 6. Six Compliance Frameworks — Deep Mapping

### Framework 1: SOC2 (Type I & Type II)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Standard** | AICPA Trust Services Criteria |
| **Categories** | Security (CC1–CC9), Availability (A1), Processing Integrity (PI1), Confidentiality (C1), Privacy (P1–P8) |
| **Control Count** | 64 Trust Services Criteria points |
| **Code-Level Signals** | Authentication mechanisms, encryption at rest/transit, access control patterns, logging coverage, error handling, session management, input validation |
| **Market Demand** | Required by 74% of enterprise buyers; prerequisite for B2B SaaS sales in US market |
| **Audit Timeline** | Type I: point-in-time (3–6 months). Type II: observation period (6–12 months) |

### Framework 2: GDPR (EU General Data Protection Regulation)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Standard** | EU Regulation 2016/679, Articles 5–49 |
| **Key Articles** | Art. 5 (principles), Art. 6 (lawful basis), Art. 13–14 (transparency), Art. 17 (right to erasure), Art. 25 (privacy by design), Art. 32 (security), Art. 33–34 (breach notification), Art. 35 (DPIA) |
| **Control Count** | 42 mapped controls |
| **Code-Level Signals** | Data flow mapping, consent mechanisms, data retention logic, deletion capabilities, encryption, pseudonymization, cross-border transfer patterns, cookie/tracking implementations |
| **Market Demand** | Mandatory for any company processing EU resident data; fines up to 4% of global annual revenue |
| **Penalty Risk** | €20M or 4% of worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher |

### Framework 3: HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Standard** | 45 CFR Parts 160, 162, 164 — Security Rule, Privacy Rule, Breach Notification Rule |
| **Safeguard Categories** | Administrative (§164.308), Physical (§164.310), Technical (§164.312), Organizational (§164.314) |
| **Control Count** | 36 mapped controls (Security Rule focus) |
| **Code-Level Signals** | PHI handling patterns, access controls, audit logging, encryption requirements, transmission security, session timeout, emergency access procedures |
| **Market Demand** | Mandatory for healthtech; required by hospitals, insurers, and healthcare enterprises before procurement |
| **Penalty Risk** | $50K–$1.9M per violation category per year; criminal penalties for knowing violations |

### Framework 4: DPDP Act (India Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Standard** | Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (India) |
| **Key Provisions** | Consent management, data fiduciary obligations, cross-border transfer restrictions, data principal rights, children's data protection, breach notification (72 hours) |
| **Control Count** | 28 mapped controls |
| **Code-Level Signals** | Consent collection/withdrawal flows, data localization patterns, age verification, purpose limitation logic, grievance redressal mechanisms |
| **Market Demand** | Growing rapidly — India's PE/VC market expanding 25% YoY; required for any company processing Indian resident data |
| **Penalty Risk** | Up to ₹250 crore (~$30M) per violation |

### Framework 5: ISO 27001:2022

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Standard** | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Information Security Management Systems |
| **Structure** | 93 Annex A controls across 4 themes: Organizational (37), People (8), Physical (14), Technological (34) |
| **Control Count** | 93 Annex A controls |
| **Code-Level Signals** | Access control implementation, cryptographic controls, secure development lifecycle, change management, vulnerability management, logging and monitoring, network security, application security |
| **Market Demand** | Globally recognized; often required alongside SOC2 for international enterprise sales; increasingly demanded in Middle East and Asia-Pacific markets |
| **Certification Timeline** | 6–12 months for initial certification |

### Framework 6: CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Standard** | California Civil Code §1798.100–§1798.199.100 |
| **Key Rights** | Right to know, right to delete, right to opt-out of sale/sharing, right to correct, right to limit use of sensitive PI, non-discrimination |
| **Control Count** | 31 mapped controls |
| **Code-Level Signals** | Data inventory mechanisms, opt-out implementation, deletion capabilities, data sale/sharing tracking, sensitive data classification, privacy notice serving, cookie consent patterns |
| **Market Demand** | Applies to businesses with >$25M revenue, >50K consumers' data, or >50% revenue from selling data; de facto US privacy standard |
| **Penalty Risk** | $2,500 per unintentional violation; $7,500 per intentional violation; private right of action for data breaches |
