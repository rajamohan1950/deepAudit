Our Approach to Confidentiality

At DeepAudit, we understand that submitting your source code for analysis requires a high degree of trust. Source code is among the most sensitive intellectual property an organization possesses. That is why every DeepAudit engagement — from a single audit to an enterprise-wide deployment — begins with a mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA).

Our NDA framework is designed to provide clear, enforceable protections for both parties, ensuring that confidential information is handled with the rigor it deserves.

What Our Standard Mutual NDA Covers

Our standard mutual NDA establishes reciprocal obligations, protecting both your information and ours. It covers the following areas:

Audit Scope Definition

Defines the specific repositories, systems, and codebases to be analyzed. Limits the engagement to the agreed-upon scope and prevents unauthorized expansion.

Source Code Confidentiality

All source code submitted for analysis is classified as Confidential Information. DeepAudit is prohibited from disclosing, copying, or using the code for any purpose other than performing the agreed-upon audit.

Report Confidentiality

Audit reports, findings, severity assessments, and remediation recommendations are treated as your Confidential Information. DeepAudit will not disclose, publish, or reference your audit results without prior written consent.

Data Handling Obligations

Specifies the encryption standards (TLS 1.3 in transit, AES-256 at rest), ephemeral processing requirements, and the prohibition on using customer code for AI model training.

Retention Limits

Source code is automatically deleted within 24 hours of audit completion. The NDA contractually enforces this timeline and provides audit verification of deletion upon request.

Mutual Obligations

The NDA is mutual — it protects both parties equally. DeepAudit's proprietary methodologies, AI models, and platform internals are also classified as Confidential Information.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the NDA required for free-tier audits?

Free-tier audits of public repositories are governed by our standard Terms of Service. For private repository audits or enterprise engagements, a signed NDA is required before any code is accessed.

How long does the NDA process take?

Most standard NDA executions are completed within 1-2 business days. Custom NDA negotiations typically take 5-10 business days depending on the complexity of requested modifications.

Can we use our own NDA template?

Yes. We are happy to review and execute your organization's standard NDA, provided it covers the minimum requirements for code handling, data processing, and confidentiality that our service requires. Our legal team will review and respond within 3 business days.

What is the standard NDA term?

Our standard mutual NDA has a confidentiality period of three (3) years from the date of disclosure, with obligations surviving the termination of the engagement for the full term.

What happens if there is a breach?

The NDA includes provisions for breach notification (within 48 hours of discovery), remediation obligations, and the right to seek injunctive relief. Both parties agree to cooperate fully in the investigation and remediation of any breach.
